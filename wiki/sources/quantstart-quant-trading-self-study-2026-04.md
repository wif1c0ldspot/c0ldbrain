# QuantStart Self-Study Plan for Quantitative Trading

## Summary

Comprehensive guide from QuantStart on how to become a quantitative trader. Covers career expectations, required skills (mathematics, programming, statistical testing), and a detailed textbook curriculum from beginner to advanced.

## Source

- URL: https://www.quantstart.com/articles/Self-Study-Plan-for-Becoming-a-Quantitative-Trader-Part-I/
- Raw: raw/articles/Quant Trading Article.md (compiled and removed)

## Key Content

### Career Expectations
- Quant research aligned with scientific hypothesis testing, not discretionary trading
- Nearly universal automation — very little discretionary input
- PhD or graduate-level Masters in quantitative field is common entry path
- 5-10 year apprenticeship to become consistently profitable
- High remuneration, intellectual environment, continuous challenges

### Required Skills
- **Mathematics:** multivariate calculus, linear algebra, probability theory
- **Statistical testing and hypothesis methodology**
- **Programming:** R, Python (data analysis libraries), or MatLab
- **Objective interpretation** of new research and rapid implementation

### Recommended Curriculum

**Phase 1: Introductory Quant Trading**
- Quantitative Trading — Ernie Chan
- Algorithmic Trading: Winning Strategies and Their Rationale — Ernie Chan

**Phase 2: Econometrics/Time Series**
- Introductory Econometrics for Finance — Brooks
- Time Series Analysis — Hamilton
- Analysis of Financial Time Series — Tsay
- Forecasting: Principles and Practice — Hyndman & Athanasopoulos (free at OTexts)

**Phase 3: Statistical/Machine Learning**
- An Introduction to Statistical Learning — James et al (ISL)
- The Elements of Statistical Learning — Hastie et al (ESL, free online)
- Machine Learning — Andrew Ng (Coursera)
- Neural Networks and Deep Learning — deeplearning.ai (Coursera)

**Core Techniques:** Multivariate Linear Regression, Logistic Regression, Resampling, Tree-Based Methods (Random Forests), Support Vector Machines (SVM), Principal Component Analysis (PCA), Clustering (K-Means, Hierarchical), Kernel Methods, Neural Networks
