# QuantStart Self-Study Plan for Quantitative Trading

## Summary

From QuantStart.com — comprehensive career guide for quantitative trading. Covers career expectations, required mathematical and programming background, and a structured textbook curriculum (this is Part I, with a Part II covering advanced topics).

## Key Content

### Career Expectations

- Quant research aligned with scientific hypothesis testing, not discretionary trading
- Processes almost universally automated — little to no discretionary input
- Common entry path: PhD or graduate Masters in quantitative field
- 5-10 year apprenticeship to become consistently profitable
- Highly intellectual environment, extremely well remunerated

### Required Background

- **Mathematics:** multivariate calculus, linear algebra, probability theory
- **Programming:** R, Python (data analysis libraries), or MatLab
- **Skills:** objective research interpretation and rapid model implementation

### Recommended Learning Path

1. **Introductory Quant Trading**
 - Quantitative Trading: How to Build Your Own Algorithmic Trading Business — Ernie Chan
 - Algorithmic Trading: Winning Strategies and Their Rationale — Ernie Chan

2. **Econometrics/Time Series Analysis**
 - Introductory Econometrics for Finance — Brooks
 - Time Series Analysis — Hamilton
 - Analysis of Financial Time Series — Tsay
 - Forecasting: Principles and Practice — Hyndman & Athanasopoulos (free at OTexts)

3. **Statistical/Machine Learning**
 - An Introduction to Statistical Learning — James et al (ISL)
 - The Elements of Statistical Learning — Hastie et al (ESL, free download)
 - Machine Learning — Andrew Ng (Coursera)
 - Neural Networks and Deep Learning — deeplearning.ai (Coursera)

### Core Techniques

Regression (multivariate, logistic), Resampling, Tree-Based Methods (Random Forests), SVM, PCA, Clustering (K-Means, Hierarchical), Kernel Methods, Neural Networks
