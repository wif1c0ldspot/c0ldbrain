## Key Facts
- [[owasp-top-10-for-llm]] — OWASP maintains an authoritative top-10 list of AI application security risks, including prompt injection, data poisoning, and supply chain vulnerabilities
- [[defense-in-depth-llm]] — Compliance frameworks advocate layered security controls rather than single-point protections
- Major frameworks include: NIST AI Risk Management Framework, EU AI Act, ISO/IEC 42001, and OWASP Top 10 for LLMs
- Compliance requires ongoing monitoring, not one-time certification

## Framework Comparison

| Framework | Scope | Status | Enforcement |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------------|
| NIST AI RMF | General AI risk management | Voluntary (US) | Self-assessment |
| EU AI Act | Risk-based classification | Mandatory (EU) | Regulatory penalties |
| ISO/IEC 42001 | AI management systems | Certification | Third-party audit |
| OWASP Top 10 LLMs | Technical security risks | Reference | Industry standard |

## Common Requirements
- Risk assessment before deployment
- Data provenance and training data documentation
- Model output monitoring and auditing
- Incident response planning for AI-specific threats
- Human-in-the-loop controls for high-risk decisions
